My Story

I was predicted to be an Architect by my nursery teacher.
An Archaeologist by many of my peers at school. On my journey to find my calling, I dipped my toes into consulting, marketing, and even politics.

Against the backdrop of the 2008 US elections, I, as an 18-year old, was obsessed with changing the world for the better. Soon after, I ventured into student politics at Edinburgh University and I realised that changing the world was tough. Much tougher than I thought.

I abandoned politics for business. I went as far afield as China to learn about international business. I took on consulting projects. I taught myself a great deal about the commercial world but a part of me longed for creativity. I had a burning inner desire to build great things.

For two years, I deliberated in applying to the web development course at General Assembly. No other experience was as great as learning to code and building your projects.

Simply put, I love to build and co-create web apps.
